New Blur Album and a thought about the song "Barbaric"

Author: Chris Athanasiadis        15/8/2023

"Blur has released their new album titled "The Ballad of Darren." I've read many comments stating that it differs quite a lot compared to their previous albums and, in general, the sound we're accustomed to from, in my opinion, the second biggest band of the Britpop scene (trailing just slightly behind Suede). The prevailing opinion is that the band's sound has always been more upbeat, robust, with a significant dose of irony towards the life of the middle-class Briton. The new album carries a tone of melancholy and is much more introspective. However, this element was never absent from Blur.


They have gifted us with tracks like "Universal" (which I believe is an anthem that should be placed alongside tracks like "Bohemian Rhapsody" and "We Are the Champions"), "No Distance Left to Run", "To the End", and others. I've listened to the album about 3-4 times, but I've mainly focused on "The Narcissist" and, especially, "Barbaric", with which I've become incredibly enamored. I think "Barbaric" perfectly depicts the end of a long-term relationship.Perhaps I draw this meaning from the song due to my own experiences, or considering Damon Albarn's breakup with Suzi Winstanley, with whom he had been for 25 years.


Either way, the meaning I extract from the song is something nobody else has sung about. Almost all love songs are centered around pain. Here, it's more ambivalent as it doesn't explain the emotion but merely expresses its absence, as well as a sense of uncertainty experienced. For me, this emotion (especially in a long-term relationship) could be security, routine, care. These are more commonly found in a long-term relationship rather than love(from the greek eros, which is closer to passion). Everyone, however, sings about love, which I believe dissipates quite quickly on its own."

A thought about Modern Gaming

Author: Chris Athanasiadis        6/7/2023

While modern gaming has undoubtedly brought exciting advancements, it's essential to reflect on the aspects that made old school gaming so beloved. One downside of modern gaming is the shift towards a more streamlined and casual experience. In the past, games often required perseverance, patience, and skill to progress. Nowadays, hand-holding tutorials, frequent checkpoints, and simplified mechanics have become prevalent, sacrificing the sense of challenge and accomplishment that defined old school gaming. This accessibility-oriented approach, while appealing to a broader audience, can lead to shorter and less fulfilling gameplay experiences for seasoned players seeking a greater sense of mastery.


Another concern with modern gaming is the increased reliance on microtransactions and downloadable content (DLC). While DLC can provide additional content and extend the lifespan of a game, it has also become a vehicle for monetization. Players often find themselves confronted with paywalls or in-game purchases that hinder their progress or artificially enhance their abilities. This model can create an uneven playing field, favoring those willing to spend additional money and detracting from the pure enjoyment and fair competition that characterized old school gaming.
